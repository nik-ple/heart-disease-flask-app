Understanding Heart Disease

Cardiovascular disease remains the leading cause of death globally. It encompasses a spectrum of conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels, ranging from coronary artery blockages and arrhythmias to congenital anomalies and valve dysfunctions.

Despite its widespread impact, many individuals remain undiagnosed until symptoms become severe. PulsePrism addresses this gap by offering a data-driven preliminary assessment using non-invasive clinical indicators—empowering users with foresight before crisis strikes.

Early detection isn't just about extending life—it’s about preserving quality of life, minimizing medical costs, and enabling timely lifestyle adjustments. PulsePrism bridges the gap between clinical complexity and public accessibility.

Inside the PulsePrism Engine

At its core, PulsePrism leverages a fine-tuned Logistic Regression model trained on the UCI Cleveland dataset. The data undergoes rigorous preprocessing: missing values are imputed, continuous features are normalized using robust scalers, and categorical variables are one-hot encoded to retain dimensional integrity.

Model selection involved benchmarking Random Forests, K-Nearest Neighbors, and Gradient Boosting alongside Logistic Regression. The final model was chosen based on ROC-AUC performance, interpretability, and consistency under k-fold cross-validation. Hyperparameters were optimized through RandomizedSearchCV, resulting in a model that balances recall and precision with 87.1% accuracy and a 0.89 F1 score.

The system is deployed as a Flask-based RESTful API, containerized using Docker for consistent environment replication and scalable deployment via cloud platforms. Render hosts the app with CI/CD pipelines triggered via GitHub Actions, ensuring reliability and fast iteration cycles.

Who Should Use PulsePrism?

PulsePrism is designed for a wide spectrum of users: individuals tracking their heart health, clinicians seeking a second-opinion screening tool, and researchers exploring clinical ML applications. Its intuitive interface ensures usability for non-technical users, while the backend architecture satisfies technical reliability standards expected in healthcare-adjacent tools.

Medical students can use PulsePrism to simulate real-world case evaluations. Hospitals and clinics can incorporate it into electronic medical records for triaging low-risk and high-risk patients. Health-tech enthusiasts can deploy it on custom dashboards, integrating wellness monitoring with wearable data.

Most importantly, PulsePrism puts actionable insight in the hands of users who need it most—early, accessible, and interpretable.
